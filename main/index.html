Our Remarkable Residents

From the sweeping savannas of Africa to the dense forests of the Northern Hemisphere, animals adapt and thrive in incredible ways.

Savanna

The African Lion

Known as the "King of the Beasts," lions are highly social felines that live in prides. They are apex predators and symbols of strength.

Plains

African Elephant

The largest living land animals on Earth. They exhibit highly intelligent behavior, deep emotional connections, and remarkable memory.

Forest

The Red Fox

Known for intelligence and adaptability, red foxes thrive globally. They feature striking reddish-brown fur and bushy tails for warmth.

Jungle

Bengal Tiger

The largest of the big cats, tigers are solitary hunters characterized by their iconic dark vertical stripes on beautiful reddish-orange fur.

Did You Know?

Lifespans

The bowhead whale can live for over 200 years, making it the longest-living mammal on Earth.

Speed

The peregrine falcon is the fastest animal in the world, capable of reaching speeds over 240 mph during a dive.

Biodiversity

More than half of the world's plant and animal species live in tropical rainforests, despite covering only 6% of Earth.
